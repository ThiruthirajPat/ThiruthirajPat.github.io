About Thiruthiraj

Experience

With over 17 years of extensive experience, Thiruthiraj has collaborated with a myriad of organisations, ranging from large corporations and SMEs to public sector bodies and NGOs, spanning countries such as France, the Netherlands, India, the UK, Mauritius, and various African countries.

His comprehensive expertise covers a broad array of fields including corporate finance, strategy, mergers and acquisitions, private equity, investor relations, competitive intelligence, corporate governance, private sector development, and trade.

Since 2006, Thiruthiraj has been delivering presentations in the corporate world, at senior executive and board levels, as well as crafting speeches and messages for C-Suite Executives. Thiruthiraj has published over 40 articles since 2004, with a focus on economics and management.

Thiruthiraj has dedicated the past five years to in-depth research and training on themes related to public speaking and personal development, including training with globally renowned experts. Between 2020 and 2023, he has earned several accolades for his motivational and inspirational speeches.

Thiruthiraj has lectured in entrepreneurship, finance, and strategic supply chain management at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels.

With his multifaceted experience and distinctive insights, Thiruthiraj stands as a Seasoned Consultant for forward-looking businesses and a well-rounded Coach for individuals aiming to elevate their public speaking skills, business acumen, and personal development.

In spite of the diversification efforts undertaken over the past decade, the structure of the Mauritian economy, though resilient in appearance, remains weak with regard to its growth potential, and more so in the light of the emerging global megatrends. The intent to position Mauritius as a globally-competitive player in the fields of life sciences, blue economy, logistics and high-value added manufacturing is unlikely to bear fruits in the near future. The financial services and ICT sectors remain biased towards low-value added activities as they continue to suffer from a critical lack of competencies. Over the 2013-2017 period, 47% of the total amount of Gross Direct Investment captured by Mauritius was geared towards real estate activities which have limited positive spill-over effects and contribution towards the reinforcement of the country’s capability to create and capture sustainable value.
